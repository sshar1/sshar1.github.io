Autonomous Robot Simulator

This is a robot simulator for the FRC 2022 game: Rapid React. I used FRC 118's CAD of their robot and animated it to work in Unity.
I created a shooting system using calculus and kinematics that will almost always have the ball land in the center of the target.
To complete the project, I implemented an A* pathfinding algorithm that computes the most optimal path to the game pieces while avoiding obstacles.

Red Robot Hackathon

My team won 1st place in CMU's annual Red Robot Hackathon. The competition consisted of creating a robot in 2 days that was capable of picking up crates and stacking them.
I CADed, 3D-printed, laser cut, and manufactured my team's robot. I also drove the robot in our 2 minute 30 second matches.
Along with winning the competition, we also won the autonomous award sponsored by Caterpillar for an autonomous routine that got maximum points in almost every match.

Nova Hackathon: Intellinote

My team won 1st place in CMU's Nova Hackathon. Our app, called Intellinote, allows users to enter a PDF of notes and calls on the OpenAI API to create questions based on the spaced repetition method. We create a collection of nodes based on word positions in the original text document to map users' understanding of different parts of their notes. This informs what questions to ask the user next.
Along with winning the competition, we also won the Everyday Impact award sponsored by Sandia Labs.

Steel Hawks: FRC 2601

I was the programming head for FRC 2601, Townsend Harris High School's robotics team. As the head, I was responsible for programming most of our robot code while organizing 30+ programming department members and teaching them Java.
My major accomplishemnts include creating our LED library, reorganizing our code to use the command-based framework, implementing control theory for the first time in my team's history, and driving our robot in every competition.
As the driver of our robot, I was responsible for strategizing with other teams, making quick decisions under a high pressure environment, and effectively communicating with my co-driver and coach during matches.

Enigma

This is an app created using Flutter. It allows students to practice for the Science Olympiad Codebusters event. It supports multiple types of ciphers and comes with an encode feature.
I implemented an algorithm that solves monoalphabetic substitution ciphers by analyzing English quadgrams and using a hill climbing algorithm.
